Biography: Tupac Shakur

Summary

Tupac Amaru Shakur (June 16, 1971 - September 13, 1996) was an American rapper and actor. He is best known for his singles, "California Love," "Dear Mama," and "Hit Em Up." On film, he is known for his work in "Juice," "Above the Rim," and "Poetic Justice."

Career & Life

Shakur began his career as MC New York in 1989. He went under the stage name 2Pac in 1991 and appeared on the single, "Same Song" by Digital Underground. Following that, he went on to create multiple gold & platinum albums that would sell millions of copies. Outside of the music world, Shakur was able to step in the film industry. Some of his roles include:

  • Juice
  • Poetic Justice
  • Above the Rim

Feud & Death

Tupac Shakur was known as the face of West Coast Rap. With that notoriety came with enemies, one of those included The Notorious B.I.G. (Christopher Wallace). Their relationship can be divided into three periods:

  1. Respect
  2. Accusation
  3. Rivalry

Wallace would turn to Shakur for business advice and looked to Shakur as an older sibling. A sudden shooting incident would bring all of that to a close when Shakur was shot in the lobby of Wallace's studio. This would change these friends to enemies until both of their untimely deaths. On the night of September 07, 1996, Shakur was on his way back to his Las Vegas hotel room when he was shot four times and succumbed to his wounds four days later. He was 25 years old.


Legacy

Following his death, Tupac's talent and work has touched many fans. Almost all of his albums, have reached gold with eight of his 11 albums making it to platinum certifications. In 2017, Tupac Shakur was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame.
