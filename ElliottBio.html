Ezekiel Elliott

Early Life

Ezekiel Elliott was born in Alton, Illinois on July 22, 1995. He went on to be a four-sport athlete in high school playing football, basketball, track, and baseball. After a successful high school career, he was touted as a 4-star prospect and ranked as the 9th best running back in the nation in 2013. He decided to enroll at Ohio State University where he committed to play football.

College Career

During his freshman year, Elliott played as the backup to current NFL player Carlos Hyde. Coming into his sophomore year, Elliott took over the role of starting running back and led Ohio State to the first-ever College Football Playoff. The following year, Elliott led the Buckeyes to the Sugar Bowl where they faced off against the #1 team in the country, the Alabama Crimson Tide. In this game, Elliott gained 230 yards on 20 rush attempts and led his team to a win in which he was named the Offensive Player of the Game. He and the Buckeyes would go on to win the championship that year. During the following year, Elliott announced his intentions to leave Ohio State and enter the 2016 NFL Draft. He finished his career at Ohio State with the second most career rushing yards in school history.


College Awards


  • Offensive MVP (2015 Sugar Bowl)
  • Offensive MVP (2015 College Football Playoff National Championship Game)
  • 2015 Graham-George Offensive Player of the Year
  • 2015 Ameche-Dayne Running Back of the Year
  • Unanimous First Team All-Big Ten
  • Big Ten Player of the Week (x2)

NFL Career

Ezekiel Elliott was taken in the first round of the 2016 NFL Draft with the number 4 overall pick by the Dallas Cowboys. Entering the league Ezekiel Elliott stated, I think I'm a guy who is going to come in and work hard from Day One. Elliott immediately became the Cowboys' starting running back. He finished his rookie year with 1,631 rushing yards, and 15 touchdowns. He was selected as a First-team All-Pro, as well as being selected for the Pro Bowl. In 2017 Elliott was suspended for 6 games of the season. Despite having missed those 6 games, he finished with 983 rushing yards and 9 total touchdowns. To date, Elliott has amassed over 6,600 rushing yards, over 2,000 receiving yards, and 58 total touchdowns. He has led the NFL in rushing yards in 2 seasons (2016 and 2018). In 2020, Elliott was listed as the 24th best player in the NFL.
